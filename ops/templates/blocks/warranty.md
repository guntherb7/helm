## 4. Warranty

### 4.1 Warranty Period

Service Provider warrants that all deliverables will be free from defects caused by Service Provider's work for a period of thirty (30) days following project completion.

### 4.2 What's Covered

During the warranty period, Service Provider will, at no additional cost:
- Fix bugs or errors in code that Service Provider created
- Correct deliverables that don't meet the specifications in this agreement
- Address functionality issues caused by Service Provider's implementation

### 4.3 What's Not Covered

Warranty does not cover:
- Issues caused by Client modifications after handoff
- Issues caused by third-party software, hosting, or infrastructure
- Issues caused by Client not following setup/maintenance instructions
- Changes to third-party platforms (e.g., WordPress updates that break compatibility)
- Performance issues caused by Client's hosting or network
- Issues caused by Client's data, content, or workflows

### 4.4 Client Responsibility

Client is responsible for:
- Testing deliverables promptly (within 5 business days of delivery)
- Reporting issues within the warranty period
- Providing sufficient information for Service Provider to reproduce issues
- Maintaining regular backups of their systems

### 4.5 Warranty Claim Process

To claim warranty coverage:
1. Client notifies Service Provider of the issue in writing
2. Service Provider investigates and determines if it's covered
3. Service Provider provides a fix or workaround
4. Client tests and confirms resolution

---

**Explanation:**

A 30-day warranty is standard and fair:

- **Client protection:** You stand behind your work for a reasonable period
- **Your protection:** You're not responsible for maintenance or long-term issues
- **Clear boundaries:** Both parties know what's covered and what's not

**Why This Matters:**

- **Builds trust:** Shows you're confident in your work
- **Limits exposure:** You're not responsible for forever support
- **Realistic:** Most bugs surface in the first month, not later
- **Client responsibility:** They have to test promptly and provide good info

---

**Variants:**

### Extended Warranty (60 days)

> Warranty period is sixty (60) days following project completion.

**Impact:** Slightly higher cost for Client, but reasonable for major projects.

### No Warranty

> Service Provider provides deliverables "as is" without warranty.

**Use only for:** Small projects, template work, or client insistence. Not recommended.

### Maintenance Contract (After Warranty)

> After the 30-day warranty period, optional maintenance is available at ${MONTHLY_RATE}/month, including bug fixes and minor updates.

**This adds post-warranty revenue.** Good for long-term client relationships.

---

**Common Objections:**

**"Why only 30 days?"**

Response: "Thirty days is industry standard. It gives you time to test and report issues, and me time to fix them. After that, bugs are usually caused by changes you've made or how your team uses it."

**"What if something breaks after 30 days?"**

Response: "If it's a bug I introduced, we can discuss it. But usually, issues after 30 days are caused by updates, changes, or how things interact with your other systems. Those are maintenance, not warranty."

**"Can we extend it to 60 days?"**

Response: "Sure, we can adjust. That would raise the project cost by $[amount] since I'm on the hook longer. Worth it for you?"
