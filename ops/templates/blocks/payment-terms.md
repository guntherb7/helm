## 2.2 Payment Schedule

**Milestone-Based Payment:**

- **Phase 1:** ${PHASE_1_VALUE} due upon signature of this agreement
- **Phase 2:** ${PHASE_2_VALUE} due upon completion of Phase 1 and client approval
- **Phase 3:** ${PHASE_3_VALUE} due upon completion of Phase 2 and client approval

**Payment Method:**

Payment should be made via {wire transfer / ACH / check} to:

{Your Payment Details}

**Payment Terms:**

Invoices are due within thirty (30) calendar days of receipt (Net 30).

**Late Payment:**

If payment is not received within thirty (30) days, Service Provider may:
- Suspend work on the project
- Charge 1.5% monthly interest on overdue balance
- Require payment in full before resuming work

**Currency:**

All payments in {CURRENCY, e.g., USD}.

---

**Variants:**

### For Faster Payment (Preferred)

Replace Net 30 with:

> Invoices are due upon receipt (Due Upon Receipt). We offer a 2% discount if payment is received within 10 days.

### For Longer Terms (Higher Risk)

If client negotiates longer terms:

> Invoices are due within forty-five (45) calendar days of receipt (Net 45).

**Note:** Longer terms increase your cash flow risk. Consider charging slightly higher rates for Net 45+.

### For Retainer Work

> Client agrees to pay a monthly retainer of ${MONTHLY_AMOUNT} on the 1st of each month. Work is billed in advance for the coming month.

### For Hourly Work

> Hourly rate: ${HOURLY_RATE} per hour. Invoiced monthly for time actually spent.

### For Fixed Price with Hourly Overage

> Fixed price: ${FIXED_AMOUNT}. Includes up to {X} hours of work. Any hours beyond that billed at ${HOURLY_RATE} per hour, invoiced monthly.

---

**Why This Matters:**

- **Upfront payment (Phase 1):** Protects you financially. Client has skin in the game.
- **Net 30 standard:** Industry standard for service providers. Reasonable for both parties.
- **Late payment clause:** Discourages late payments without being punitive.
- **Clear payment method:** Prevents confusion about how to pay.

---

**Common Negotiation Points:**

- Client asks for Net 45 or Net 60
  - Counter: "Net 30 is our standard. For longer terms, we'd adjust pricing."
  - Compromise: Accept Net 45 if client is established/trustworthy. Avoid Net 60 unless high-value.

- Client wants to pay upon completion of all phases
  - Counter: "Milestone-based protects both of us. Each phase has clear deliverables and payment trigger."
  - Don't accept all-payment-at-end unless client is very well-known/trusted.

- Client wants monthly retainer instead of project
  - This is fine if pricing reflects it. Retainer is usually 20-30% higher than equivalent project hourly rate (stability premium).
