# Limitation of Liability Block

Insert this block into Section 5 of contract-structure.md

---

## 5. Limitation of Liability

### 5.1 Cap on Liability

Neither party will be liable for more than the total amount paid under this agreement.

In no event will either party be liable for more than ${TOTAL_VALUE} (the total contract value), regardless of the nature of the claim.

### 5.2 Excluded Damages

Except in case of willful misconduct, neither party is liable for:
- Lost profits or revenue
- Lost data or business interruption
- Indirect, incidental, or consequential damages
- Punitive or exemplary damages
- Any damages arising from decisions made based on the work

This applies even if the other party has been advised of the possibility of such damages.

### 5.3 Data Loss

Client is responsible for maintaining regular backups of their systems and data. Service Provider is not liable for any data loss, regardless of cause.

### 5.4 Indemnification

Service Provider will indemnify Client against claims that Service Provider's work infringes third-party intellectual property rights, provided that:
- Client has not modified the work
- Client has not used the work in combination with unauthorized third-party software
- Client has notified Service Provider promptly of any claim

---

**Explanation:**

This protects both parties but especially protects you (small business) from catastrophic liability:

- **Your protection:** You won't lose your house if something goes wrong
- **Client still protected:** You're liable up to what they paid you
- **Reality:** Exclude indirect damages because freelancers can't absorb million-dollar claims
- **Fair:** Both parties understand the limits

**Why This Matters:**

- **Critical for freelancers:** A single lawsuit can bankrupt a small operation
- **Insurance-backed:** Most E&O policies require these clauses
- **Industry standard:** Every professional services contract has limitation of liability
- **Risk management:** Both parties know the worst-case exposure

---

**What This Covers:**

✅ Client gets: Coverage up to contract value (reasonable)
✅ You get: Protection from catastrophic claims
✅ Both parties: Clear understanding of risk allocation

---

**Variants:**

### Uncapped Liability (Don't Agree)

> Service Provider's liability is not limited.

**Risk:** Unlimited exposure. Don't accept this.

### Higher Cap (for larger projects)

> Service Provider's liability is capped at twice the total contract value (${2X_VALUE}).

**Use for:** High-risk projects where Client demands more. Charge more in these cases.

### Insurance-Based

> Service Provider maintains professional liability insurance of ${AMOUNT}. Service Provider's liability is capped at insurance policy limits or contract value, whichever is greater.

**This shows you're professional and insured.** If you have E&O insurance, include it.

---

**Common Objections:**

**"This doesn't protect us if something goes really wrong."**

Response: "It protects both of us. You can recover the money you paid me, plus any insurance I have. Beyond that, I'm a small operation and can't absorb million-dollar claims. This is standard across the industry."

**"What if your mistake costs us $500,000?"**

Response: "That's covered up to what you paid me—the project cost. If the impact is much higher than the fee, that's a sign the project needs enterprise-level support with insurance. For work at this price point, this limitation is standard and fair."

**"Can you remove the 'indirect damages' part?"**

Response: "No. That's non-negotiable. Indirect damages (lost profits, business interruption) are why professional liability insurance exists. Without this clause, I'd need expensive insurance that would drive your project cost way up."

---

**What You Should Always Include:**

1. ✅ Cap on total liability ($ amount)
2. ✅ Exclusion of indirect/consequential damages
3. ✅ Client responsible for backups
4. ✅ Indemnification clause (you indemnify them for IP claims)

**Don't Give These Up:**

- ❌ Don't accept unlimited liability
- ❌ Don't agree to pay for "lost profits"
- ❌ Don't take responsibility for Client's backups
- ❌ Don't agree to indemnify for Client's modifications

---

**Template Variables to Replace:**

- ${TOTAL_VALUE} → Same as contract total
- ${2X_VALUE} → For higher cap variants
- ${AMOUNT} → Insurance policy limit if applicable
