# Tax Preparation

Tax timeline, checklist, deductions, and filing information.

## Tax Filing Timeline

### January – February (Year-End)

**January 31 deadline:**
- Collect all 1099s from clients
- Bank and credit card statements downloaded
- Invoice records compiled
- Expense receipts organized

**February:**
- Meet with accountant
- Prepare for tax return
- Gather additional documentation if needed

**March – April:**

**April 15:**
- Tax return filed (or extension filed)
- Final tax payment due (or refund received)
- Quarterly estimated tax Q1 due (if self-employed)

### May – December (Throughout Year)

**Ongoing:**
- Save receipts (business expenses)
- Track mileage (if claiming home office)
- Monitor income vs expenses
- Set aside quarterly tax payments

**June 15:**
- Q2 estimated tax due

**September 15:**
- Q3 estimated tax due

**January 15 (Next Year):**
- Q4 estimated tax due

### Professional Services

- [ ] Accountant/bookkeeper fees

- [ ] Freelance designer/developer (if subcontracting)
- [ ] Virtual assistant services

### Home Office

Calculate as:
```
(Dedicated office space in sq ft / Total home sq ft) × Monthly utilities & internet

Example:
150 sq ft office / 2,000 sq ft home = 7.5%
$150 internet + $100 utilities = $250
$250 × 7.5% = $18.75/month = $225/year
```

Deductible:
- [ ] Utilities (electricity, heat, water - prorated)
- [ ] Internet/phone (prorated if business use)
- [ ] Rent or mortgage interest (if renting/mortgaging)
- [ ] Home insurance (prorated)
- [ ] Home maintenance (if separate office)

NOT deductible:
- Personal portion of utilities
- Meals at home
- Commute

### Business Expenses

- [ ] Client meetings (travel)
- [ ] Conference or training travel
- [ ] Meals related to business (50% deductible)
- [ ] Parking or transportation for business
- [ ] Business vehicle mileage (track in mileage log)

### Insurance

- [ ] Professional liability (E&O insurance)
- [ ] General liability (if applicable)
- [ ] Health insurance premiums (self-employed)

## Quarterly Estimated Tax Calculation

For self-employed individuals without W-2 income:

**Step 1: Estimate annual net income**

```
Projected 2026 revenue:        $60,000
Less: Business expenses:       -$5,000
Less: Half of self-employment: -$4,239
Estimated taxable income:      $50,761
```

**Step 2: Calculate tax owed**

Self-employed tax calculation:
```
Net income × 92.35% = Self-employment income
Self-employment income × 15.3% = SE Tax (~$7,800)

Federal income tax estimate: 22% bracket = ~$11,300
State income tax estimate: Varies by state (~$2,500)

Total annual tax: ~$21,600
Quarterly estimated: $21,600 ÷ 4 = $5,400/quarter
```

**Step 3: Pay quarterly**

- **Q1 (Jan-Mar):** Pay by April 15
- **Q2 (Apr-Jun):** Pay by June 15
- **Q3 (Jul-Sep):** Pay by September 15
- **Q4 (Oct-Dec):** Pay by January 15 (next year)

**Payment methods:**
- IRS Direct Pay: https://www.irs.gov/payments
- Electronic Federal Tax Payment System (EFTPS)
- Credit/debit card (fees apply)
- Mail: Form 1040-ES with check

## Tax Forms You'll Need

**If self-employed (1099 income):**
- [ ] Form 1040 (Personal income tax return)
- [ ] Schedule C (Self-employment income)
- [ ] Schedule SE (Self-employment tax)
- [ ] Form 1040-ES (Quarterly estimated tax payments)

**If you have an LLC (elected as S-Corp):**
- [ ] Form 1120-S (S-Corporation return)
- [ ] Schedule K-1 (Your share of income)
- [ ] Form 940/941 (Payroll taxes, if paying yourself)

**Always:**
- [ ] Receipts and documentation for deductions (keep 7 years)
- [ ] Bank statements (1 year minimum)
- [ ] Invoice records (7 years recommended)

## Philosophy

Taxes don't have to be stressful. The key is staying organized throughout the year:
- Keep receipts
- Track expenses monthly
- Update accounts.md regularly
- Save for quarterly payments

When January comes, you'll have everything your accountant needs and avoid the April crunch.

**Set a recurring reminder:** First Friday of each month to review expenses and receipts.
