| Hours/Week | Monthly Cost | Commitment |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| 10 hours | $2,000 | 3 months minimum |
| 15 hours | $2,800 | 3 months minimum |
| 20 hours | $3,500 | 3 months minimum |

**Included in retainer:**
- Ongoing support and bug fixes
- Small enhancements (under 4 hours/week)
- Priority response time (24-48 hours)

**Not included:**
- Large new features (treated as change order)
- Significant redesigns
- Third-party integrations

## Pricing Philosophy

**Value-based pricing:**
- Price is based on value delivered, not hours spent
- Fixed-price projects with clear scope
- Retainers for ongoing relationships

**Minimum pricing:**
- Nothing under $150/hour equivalent
- Minimum project: $5,000 (smaller projects are hourly)
- Retainer minimum: 10 hours/week @ $200/hr

**Premium situations:**
- Rush projects: +25% (expedited timeline)
- Difficult clients: +15% (extra communication/review cycles)
- Technical complexity: +15% (custom integrations, legacy systems)

## What Worked (Lessons Learned)

✅ **Fixed pricing for clear-scope projects**
- Clients prefer knowing total cost upfront
- Easier to sell (price is a number, not open-ended)
- Better margin than hourly for us (finish faster = higher effective rate)

✅ **$5,000 minimum for projects**
- Anything smaller doesn't cover sales, meetings, admin overhead
- Clients below this threshold often have difficult decision-making
- Better to turn down small projects than lose margin

✅ **Retainers for ongoing clients**
- Much more predictable revenue
- 3-month minimum prevents churn
- Clients that retainer well are typically good partners

✅ **Salesforce expertise premium**
- CRM integrations are high-value and specialized
- Can charge 15-20% premium for this expertise
- Acme Corp example: Website + Salesforce = $15k (might have been $10k for website alone)

---

## What Didn't Work (Warnings)

❌ **Hourly rate for open-ended projects**
- Scope creep is inevitable
- "Just one more thing" kills margin
- Clients unhappy about final bill

❌ **Discounting for "exposure" or early clients**
- Never gained additional work from discounted project
- Set bad price expectation with client
- Did margin for no reason

❌ **Taking very small projects ($2-3k)**
- Admin overhead is same whether project is $2k or $10k
- Low-paying clients are often difficult
- Not worth the stress

❌ **Custom hourly billing**
- Clients hate hourly billing
- Incentivizes slow work (bad for both)
- Hard to predict final cost (bad for client budgeting)

## Pricing By Client Type

### SMB (Small Business, $2-5M revenue)

- **Budget:** $3,000 – $7,000
- **Decision maker:** Owner (fast decisions)
- **Payment:** Often Net 45 (slower payment)
- **Approach:** Fixed price, clear scope, payment terms upfront

### Mid-Market ($5-50M revenue)

- **Budget:** $5,000 – $20,000
- **Decision maker:** Department head or manager
- **Payment:** Net 30 typical (better)
- **Approach:** Proposal with phased approach, Net 30 payment terms

### Enterprise ($50M+)

- **Budget:** $10,000+ (willing to pay)
- **Decision maker:** Multiple (slow decisions)
- **Payment:** Net 30-45 (but reliable)
- **Approach:** Proposal, contract review (4 weeks typical), Net 30, may want retainer

**Acme Corp is mid-market:** $15k budget, Net 30 payment, phased approach, good for our pricing.

## Pricing Negotiation Strategy

**Never discount price. Add value instead.**

If client asks "Can you do this for less?":

> "I can't reduce price—my rate reflects the value you get. But I can reduce scope:
> - Skip [feature] in Phase 1?
> - Defer [deliverable] to Phase 2?
> - Use a template instead of custom design?
>
> What's your core need? Let's solve that at this price point."

**Never split the difference.** It trains the client to negotiate further.

**Stand on value, not cost.**

> "This integrates with your Salesforce and connects to your sales team's workflow. That's why it's $5k, not $2.5k. The value is there; the price reflects it."

## Pricing Calculator

Quick reference for pricing new projects:

```
Website rebuild:
- Base: $5,000
- + Complexity (custom design): $3,000-7,000
- + Integrations (Salesforce, etc.): $2,000-5,000
- Total estimate: $5,000-17,000

Rule of thumb:
- Simple: $5,000
- Medium: $8,000-12,000
- Complex: $15,000+

For rushed timelines: Add 25%
For difficult scope: Add 15%
For retainer clients (discount): -10%
```
